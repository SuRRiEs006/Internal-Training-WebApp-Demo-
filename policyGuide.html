Latham Bakery HR Policies

1. Code of Conduct

At Latham Bakery, we expect all employees to maintain the highest standards of conduct and professionalism. This includes treating colleagues and customers with respect, adhering to company policies, and maintaining a positive and productive work environment.

2. Equal Opportunity Employment

Latham Bakery is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate against employees or job applicants based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by law.

3. Workplace Safety

The safety of our employees is a top priority. Employees must follow all safety protocols and report any hazards or injuries immediately to their supervisor. Regular safety training sessions will be conducted to ensure all staff are knowledgeable about workplace safety standards.

4. Attendance and Punctuality

Regular attendance and punctuality are essential to the smooth operation of Latham Bakery. Employees are expected to report to work on time and adhere to their scheduled shifts. In case of illness or emergency, employees must notify their supervisor as soon as possible.

5. Dress Code

All employees are required to maintain a clean and professional appearance while on duty. Uniforms, if provided, must be worn during work hours, and personal hygiene must be maintained. Any specific dress code requirements will be communicated by the management.

6. Confidentiality

Employees must maintain the confidentiality of all company information, including recipes, customer data, and proprietary processes. Sharing confidential information outside of Latham Bakery without authorization is strictly prohibited.

7. Harassment and Discrimination

Latham Bakery has a zero-tolerance policy for any form of harassment or discrimination. Employees are encouraged to report any incidents of harassment or discrimination to their supervisor or HR representative. All reports will be investigated promptly and thoroughly.

8. Substance Abuse

The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol on company premises is strictly prohibited. Employees must not report to work under the influence of drugs or alcohol. Violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

9. Performance Reviews

Regular performance reviews will be conducted to provide feedback, set goals, and discuss career development opportunities. Employees are encouraged to actively participate in these reviews and to seek feedback for continuous improvement.

10. Leave Policies

Employees are entitled to various types of leave, including vacation, sick leave, and personal leave. Requests for leave must be submitted in advance and approved by the supervisor. Detailed information about leave entitlements and procedures will be provided in the employee handbook.

11. Benefits

Latham Bakery offers a range of benefits to eligible employees, including health insurance, retirement plans, and employee discounts. Information about eligibility and enrollment procedures will be provided upon hiring and during open enrollment periods.

12. Disciplinary Actions

Failure to adhere to company policies and standards may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Disciplinary actions will be documented, and employees will be given the opportunity to address any issues raised.

13. Open Door Policy

Latham Bakery encourages open communication between employees and management. Employees are welcome to discuss any concerns, suggestions, or feedback with their supervisor or HR representative at any time.
